Avi Lewis, a 58-year-old filmmaker, activist, and former television host, won the NDP leadership race on the first ballot with 56% of votes cast. Lewis received 39,734 of 70,930 valid votes, defeating Heather McPherson (20,899 votes) and three other candidates.

Lewis pledged to revitalize the party, stating: "Canada, mark your calendar: The NDP comeback starts now."

His election drew immediate criticism from NDP leaders in Saskatchewan and Alberta who oppose his calls for oil and gas pipeline levies. The Centre for Israel and Jewish Affairs also criticized Lewis over his pro-Palestinian statements and characterization of the Gaza conflict as genocide.

The NDP faced historic decline going into the convention, winning only 7 seats and 6.3% in the previous election under Jagmeet Singh. The party holds 6 parliamentary seats, below the 12-seat threshold for official status.
